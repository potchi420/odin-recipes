Recipes for Bistek Tagalog

Description

Bistek Tagalog, also known as Filipino Beef Steak, is a classic dish made with thinly sliced beef marinated in a mixture of soy sauce, calamansi juice, and garlic, then cooked with onions. It is a savory and tangy dish that is often served with steamed rice.

  • 1 lb beef sirloin, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice (or lemon juice)
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 large onion, sliced into rings
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup water

Steps

  1. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, calamansi juice, minced garlic, and ground black pepper. Add the sliced beef and marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Remove the beef from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and cook the beef in batches until browned. Set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the sliced onions and cook until they are soft and translucent.
  4. Return the beef to the skillet and pour in the reserved marinade along with 1/2 cup of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for about 10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  5. Adjust seasoning if necessary. Serve hot with steamed rice.
